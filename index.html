About me

Everyday technology creates new opportunities and makes the unthinkable possible.

Technologies such as the Internet, mobile devices, cloud computing and artificial intelligence have tremendous impact in the world.

My role is to understand how they work and how to apply them to solve specific challenges for our clients.

I am managing director of codeScrum, a company we founded with my brother Miguel.

Consultant at the Ministry of Justice

MoJ was the first major Government Department with in the UK Government to have a large (120+ people) digital team working with Agile Methodologies.

Jairo worked as a technical consultant for almost a year from 2013 to 2004. Jairo led the development of the latest iterations of the Court Finder and Tribunal Decisions portals among several other projects.

As part of the MoJ, Jairo won second place at the Government Hackaton sponsored by MongoDB, more information about this event is available in the MoJ Digital blog here.

Software Provider for The Stage Castings

Established in 1880, The Stage provides news and services for the UK entertainment and performing arts industry.

Jairo led the development for a new digital product named Castings and successfully transitioned the product to the client for launch and operation with their own internal team.

As part of this role, Jairo engaged with top senior management at the client and led his team of 3 developers to complete the total implementation of the new system.

Jairo did important open source contributors to the gem that integrates the Sage payment gateway to a ruby application.

Organiser for the London Ruby Unconference

Starting in 2016, Jairo has been the organiser of the London Ruby Unconference, a one-day event with multiple tracks, with discussion round tables and workshops to learn specific skills about software programming with Ruby.

Jairo also organises monthly events such as the Ruby Hacknight and Ruby Business UK Meetups, aimed at ruby developers the first one and CTOs and Directors of companies running with Ruby for the second.

Jairo has been a volunteer coach in several editions of Rails Girls that teaches women how to code, the London Computer Coding Club Wizzie Wizzie that teaches kids how to code and CodeBar.io that teaches underrepresented people how to program.
